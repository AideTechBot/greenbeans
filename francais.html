Qui sommes nous?

À greengarden, nous voulons que tous se sente partie de la famille! Apprend à connaître qui nous sommes comme organization et comment nous voulons renforcer le sentiment de communauté chez les familles au Canada.

CROYANCES

Notre devise à GreenGarden est “Guidage, croissance, grandeur”. Elle baseé sur la croyance qu’en guidant les familles à apprendre à devenir auto-durables et en promouvant un style de vie sain on verra un développement dans toutes les communautés. Alors, notre organisation créera une communauté de grandeur.

BUTS

Notre mission est d’aider les foyers à faible revenu à travers le Canada à avoir accès à la nourriture saine afin de mener une vie meilleure et plus saine.

COMMENT

Nous soutenons les familles à faible revenu en:

  • Offrant de la nourriture saine à des prix abordables
  • Créant un système de jardinage efficace et durable
  • Aidant les foyers à poursuivre des choix alimentaires plus sains
  • Utilisant des technologies avancées pour augmenter la reproduction sur des terres limitées
  • Réduisant le stigmate en intégrant le quartier au complet à l’utilisation du marché

Histoire

L'histoire de notre humble début

  • Le 11 Juillet, 2016

    SHAD Valley

    GreenGarden a été fondé par six SHAD’s de SHAD Waterloo. Ils étaient tous poussés par la question: “Comment pourrions nous aider les foyers à faible revenu à avoir accès à la nourriture saine sans fournir de l’aide financière directe?” L’idée était un mélange de plusieurs facteurs différents concernant l'insécurité alimentaire. Il y avait plusieurs sujets dont nous étions intéressés comme la stigmatisation autour des familles utilisant les banques alimentaires ainsi que grandir des aliments d’une manière durable. C’est en essayant de trouver une solution pour ces problèmes qu’est né GreenGarden.

  • Le 3 Aout 2016

    Post SHAD

    Suivant la naissance de GreenGarden, plusieurs des SHADs de Waterloo se sont joints au groupe de GreenGarden avec des intérêts et passions similaires en tête. Au total, vingt autres SHADs ont joint l'équipe de six. à travers le mois d’Aout, ces SHADs on aussi mis leurs brillants esprits et talents au travail avec le but d'améliorer l’idee originale de GreenGarden. Rainwater Irrigation System

Rainwater is both a renewable and sustainable water source. Using rainwater as the water source for gardening will help to save money on utilities every month, thus reducing the need to put costs into maintenance.

Whenever it rains, the water will be collected in barrels at the back of the building. Then using the system that has been installed, when needed, the water will be pumped into the drip irrigation system. On our vertical gardening stands, there will be an irrigation system installed at the top. The water will drip slowly down all the shelves and the system will make sure the soil stays continuously moist but not overwatered.

Some of the plants may need to be watered by hand depending on the type of plant it is. There will also be a connection to the main water supply in the case that there isn’t enough rainwater to water the plants for the day. GreenGarden will be teaming up with a irrigation company to install/develop this technology.

Vertical Gardening

Vertical gardening is a method of gardening where plants are grown along the walls in a vertical direction. Not only does this method of gardening increase the amount of plants you can grow by using space vertically rather than horizontally, it also looks aesthetically pleasing. Plants growing around the walls can have easier access to sunlight as well.

Almost all types of vegetables and fruits can be grown vertically. Some examples are, tomatoes, kale, radishes, cucumbers, peas, melons and passion fruit. In a typical vertical garden, plants are usually grown on the south, however, using technologies such as light shelves we are able to spread light throughout the room. Also, for a successful garden, the soil needs to be deep and well drained thus, the drip rainwater irrigation system works well with our vertical gardening system.

There will be vertical gardens lining the second floor of the building where there are polycarbonate walls and light shelves installed.

Cold Frames

Cold frames are simple structures which use solar energy and insulation to create a mini warm climate for plants to grow in the winter. These are very easy to use, make and are also inexpensive.

There are four reasons why someone might use a cold frame. The first is to overwinter plants, which means to keep plants in a sleeping mode until the climatic conditions are ready for them to grow again. This is ideally used for tropical plants which don’t grown in the winter. The second is to let seedlings have an early start to grow. By keeping them in the cold frame during the winter then results in them having an early growth period to be ready by the spring. The third reason is let plants get used to being out in the harsh climates rather than in the comfort of home. The final reason is to extend plants to last after frost falls so they can be harvested at a later date.

The cold frames at GreenGarden are rectangular wooden prisms which have a clear glass top that allow light to enter. These can be homemade as well as bought. They will be used during the winter time to get ready for the next growing season.

Light Shelves

Light Shelves are horizontal light-reflecting overhangs that allow daylight to penetrate deep into a building. The purpose is for even redistribution of light throughout the garden, and to reduce the use of artificial light during peak hours of the day. They bounce the light upward improving the distribution and offsets unwanted concentration heat loads.

The shelves are at an adjustable angle that automatically changes through the day and they are on a tilt so there is easier maintenance. A downward tilt increases the penetration of light and an upward tilt avoids glare and increases daylight at a low sun angle such as in the winter.

Light shelves are usually made out of aluminum carbonate or polycarbonate with a reflective pain and have a rough white surface that maximizes the reflective surface and scatters light at different angles for diverse coverage.

They are used specifically in the winter to compensate for limited sun exposure and are built onto tall windows to eliminate the possibility of glares.

Building Specifics

GreenGarden locations are made with much consideration to the resources available in each individual subsidized housing community. Some locations may not have the same layout as many of the other locations because of space and size.

Most locations; however, have three floors. The ground floor and basement are concrete and are used to store and sell food to customers. The rest of the building is then made out of polycarbonate which is a strong material that provides sound walls while being semi transparent. It’s also a material mainly used in greenhouses.

The building contains a curtain wall infrastructure so that weight is evenly distributed throughout the structure. Thus the walls will not hold most of the weight of the items on top of the building, and the overall load of the building can be a little heavier because the walls are not the main source of strength.

Many buildings can be renovated to include a curtain wall infrastructure, however, depending on the location of the subsidized housing community there may be edits made to this general design.
